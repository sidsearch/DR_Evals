# The 2008 Financial Crisis: Causes and Economic Consequences

## Overview
The 2008 financial crisis was the worst economic disaster since the Great Depression. It was triggered by a collapse in the US housing market.

## Main Causes
- Banks gave out too many risky mortgages (subprime lending)
- These mortgages were bundled into complex financial products called CDOs
- Credit rating agencies gave these products high ratings even though they were risky
- When housing prices dropped, many homeowners defaulted on their loans
- This caused big banks like Lehman Brothers to fail

## Economic Consequences
- GDP fell in most developed countries
- Unemployment rose sharply, reaching about 10% in the US
- Governments had to bail out banks using taxpayer money
- The crisis spread globally, affecting Europe and developing countries
- Recovery took many years, with some regions still affected a decade later

## Conclusion
The 2008 crisis showed that financial deregulation and complex financial instruments can create systemic risk. New regulations like Dodd-Frank were introduced to prevent a repeat.
